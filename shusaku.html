Hon'inbō Shūsaku

"The Man Behind The Fuseki Revolution"








  • Shusaku's Life

  • 1829: Birth of Torajiro Kuwahara
  • 1834: Torajiro's talent is unveiled by Hashimoto Yoshibei
  • 1835: Torajiro plays with Lord Asano and is tutored by Abbot Hoshin
  • 1837: Torajiro plays against Ito Showa before travelling to Edo to become a student at Honinbo
  • 1840: Torajiro visits his hometown, Tonoura.
  • 1841: Torajiro returns to Edo as a student, changes name to Shusaku, and is promoted to 2-dan
  • 1842: Shusaku get's promoted to 3-dan
  • 1843: Shusaku develops shusaku fuseki and is also promoted to 4-dan
  • 1844: Shusaku visits home again, Tonoura, and wins 3 out of 4 games against Ito Showa
  • 1846: Shusaku leaves home again and plays in Osaka winning many games, he's promoted to 5th
    dan and he is offered to become Shuwa's heir which he refuses.
  • 1847: Shusaku agrees to become Shuwa's heir after Asano release their claim on him.
  • 1848: Shusaku becomes the Honinbo heir and has an audience with the shogun, marries Hana,
    get's promoted to 6-dan
  • 1850: Shusaku travels home for 6 months.
  • 1851: Shusaku plays a nijubango against Sekiyama Sendaju in Matsuyo, wins 13-7.
  • 1853: Shusaku does a sanjubango vs Ota Yuzo and is promoted to 7th dan
  • 1854: Party is held for Shusaku's promotion, major earthquake cancels O-konami go
  • 1855: Large earthquake cancels all castle games
  • 1857: Shusaku travels home again
  • 1861: Shusaku plays his last 2 castle games
  • 1862: The death of Shusaku
Shusaku the Modest

When shusaku was granted the rank of 5-dan he was requested to become the heir to Shuwa. To many's surprise he initially turned down the role due to his concientous and courteous ways. Initially he felt obliged towards the Asano Clan who had been financially supporting him, to other benefactors in Onomichi, and his own parents... Such humility!

It Was The Worst of Times...

In midsummer of 1862 an epidemic of cholera broke out in Edo, a large number of Honinbo students caught the disease. Against all of Shuwa's protests Shusaku devoted himself to nursing the patients and in turn became inflicted himself. After such a hard year of fasting and stress he stood no chance against the insidious bacteria. After 7 days of agony he succumbed to the illness.
